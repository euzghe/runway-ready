# Deck 4 — Outfit Detail Cards (×30, 15 clean + 15 with planted flaws)

**ISO/IEC 29119-3 mapping:** Test Case Specification
**ISO fields preserved on every card:** *Identifier · Objective · Pre-conditions · Inputs · Expected Result · Post-conditions · Traceability ID*
**Function in play:** The atomic testable unit. Plays under an active Theme. **Half of this deck is flawed by design** — that is what the Inspection sub-step exists to catch.

> **Important for the playtester:** the flaw on each flawed card is *real and specific*. The "Originating Field" line at the bottom of the answer key tells you what the rival should have been able to spot. Do not reveal the answer key to opponents during play.

## Why these matter (educational intent)

These 15 flaw types cover the **defect classes that ISO/IEC 29119-3 implicitly defines** for Test Case Specifications: missing fields, ambiguous fields, untestable fields, internally inconsistent fields, broken traceability, scope violations, identifier collisions, and self-referential definitions. A learner who plays an entire game of *Runway Ready* will physically encounter every one of these classes in their hand. The misconception "test cases are simple input → output pairs" cannot survive contact with this deck.
