# Deck 1 — Brand Identity Cards (×8)

**ISO/IEC 29119-3 mapping:** Organizational Test Strategy
**ISO fields preserved on every card:** *Test policy reference · Scope of applicability · Generic risks · Global rules*
**Function in play:** Sets a global constraint that every later card must satisfy. One Brand Identity is active for the entire game.

---

## BI-01 — Maison Solère

- *Test policy reference:* Sustainability Charter §1
- *Scope of applicability:* All collections, all seasons
- *Generic risks:* Greenwashing accusations, supply-chain delays
- **Global rule:** Every Outfit must use only sustainable materials. An Outfit listing leather or synthetic fur is invalid.

## BI-02 — Atelier Nox

- *Test policy reference:* Couture-only Mandate
- *Scope of applicability:* Exclusively haute couture lines
- *Generic risks:* Cost overruns, fitter fatigue
- **Global rule:** No more than 6 Outfits per Roadmap. Each Outfit must list at least 2 hand-finished elements.

## BI-03 — Studio Heliograph

- *Test policy reference:* Minimalism Manifesto v3
- *Scope of applicability:* Ready-to-wear, Spring/Summer
- *Generic risks:* Visual monotony, low retail appeal
- **Global rule:** No Outfit may declare more than one accessory.

## BI-04 — House of Vermeil

- *Test policy reference:* Heritage Preservation Code
- *Scope of applicability:* Bridal and ceremonial only
- *Generic risks:* Tradition vs. modernity tension
- **Global rule:** Every Outfit must include at least one element marked "heritage" in its description.

## BI-05 — Kuro & Co.

- *Test policy reference:* Avant-Garde Doctrine
- *Scope of applicability:* Concept shows, Fashion Week only
- *Generic risks:* Public misunderstanding, press backlash
- **Global rule:** At least one Outfit per round must intentionally violate one Roadmap constraint (artistic statement). That Outfit must declare which constraint it violates in its Pre-conditions field.

## BI-06 — Linea Pura

- *Test policy reference:* Functional Wear Charter
- *Scope of applicability:* Performance-wear, all seasons
- *Generic risks:* Aesthetic dismissal, sizing complaints
- **Global rule:** Every Outfit's Expected Result must include a movement constraint (e.g., "must permit full arm extension").

## BI-07 — Maison Aurore

- *Test policy reference:* Inclusivity Standard 2026
- *Scope of applicability:* All collections
- *Generic risks:* Tokenism criticism, sizing inconsistency
- **Global rule:** Every Roadmap must declare at least 3 distinct model sizes. Every Outfit must list a size compatible with the Roadmap's declared sizes.

## BI-08 — Atelier Bruté

- *Test policy reference:* Brutalist Aesthetic Code
- *Scope of applicability:* Conceptual menswear
- *Generic risks:* Wearability, retail viability
- **Global rule:** Every Outfit must use a single dominant material (e.g., wool, denim, canvas). Mixed-material Outfits are invalid.

---

## Why these matter (educational intent)

Each Brand Identity card models how a real Organizational Test Strategy constrains downstream documents. A learner who plays *Maison Solère* and then drafts an Outfit using "polyester satin" has just produced a real-world ISO violation: **the test case contradicts the organizational strategy**. The game catches this in the Inspection sub-step of Rehearsal — and the rival who catches it scores Inspection Points.
