# Deck 7 — Event / Action Cards (×12)

**ISO/IEC 29119-3 mapping:** Test environment factors, project risks, audit triggers
**Function in play:** Drawn at the start of each player's turn (full game) or skipped (Lite variant). Events simulate the volatility of real testing projects without making outcomes random.

---

## EV-01 — "Budget Cut"

Discard 1 accessory from your active Roadmap's Resources field. Any Outfit that depended on it must be Revised or fail.

## EV-02 — "Auditor Visit"

All players must read their active Brand Identity card aloud. Any Outfit currently on a mat that violates it is immediately marked for Inspection.

## EV-03 — "Press Embargo Lifted"

Reveal one opponent's hand of Outfit Detail cards. They have 30 sec to swap one out before play resumes.

## EV-04 — "Schedule Crunch"

Your turn is shortened to 90 sec total. If you fail to complete all 5 phases, any unstarted phase is treated as a hierarchy violation.

## EV-05 — "Model No-Show"

Discard one Outfit from your mat. You may replace it with one from your hand at no penalty if it satisfies the active Theme.

## EV-06 — "Last-Minute Sponsor"

Add 1 accessory allowance to your Roadmap. You may use it on a single Outfit this turn.

## EV-07 — "Critic in the Front Row"

During Inspection this turn, opponents have 90 sec instead of 60 sec, and Inspection Points are doubled.

## EV-08 — "Wardrobe Mix-Up"

Swap one Outfit Detail card with the player on your left, hand-to-hand, before Specify phase begins.

## EV-09 — "Power Failure on Lighting Rig"

Any Outfit whose Expected Result depends on lighting (e.g., Liquid Light theme) automatically fails this turn unless explicitly Revised.

## EV-10 — "Fitter on Strike"

You may not Revise this turn. Any flaw caught during Inspection becomes an automatic Wardrobe Malfunction.

## EV-11 — "Surprise Theme Pivot"

Discard your active Collection Theme and draw a new one. All Outfits already on your mat must be re-checked against the new Theme.

## EV-12 — "Regulatory Audit"

For one round, any Outfit missing a Traceability ID is automatically marked as a defect, regardless of execution roll.

---

## Why these matter (educational intent)

Real test projects are not closed systems. Schedules slip, budgets get cut, auditors arrive unannounced. Event cards simulate this — but **they never override traceability**. Even under a Schedule Crunch, the player still has to write valid documentation; they just have less time to do it. The lesson: documentation discipline is the **first thing that breaks under pressure**, and *Runway Ready* makes that visible.
