# Deck 6 — Wardrobe Malfunction Cards (×16)

**ISO/IEC 29119-3 mapping:** Defect Report
**ISO fields preserved on every card:** *Defect ID · Severity · Description · Status · Impact · Originating field*
**Function in play:** Drawn whenever an Outfit fails Rehearsal. **Each card explicitly names the ISO field that produced the defect.** The player must locate that field on their own mat and verbalise the flaw to resolve.

---

## WM-01 — "Heel snapped on the runway"

- *Defect ID:* WM-01
- *Severity:* High
- *Description:* Model's heel detached at second step.
- *Status:* Open until Revised
- *Impact:* Outfit fails; rehearsal halted.
- **Originating field to locate:** *Pre-conditions* on the Outfit Detail card. (e.g., wrong shoe size or unsuitable shoe declared.)

## Why these matter (educational intent)

Real-world defect reports are useless when they say "it broke". They are useful when they name the **originating artefact**. Every Wardrobe Malfunction in this deck forces the player to perform that exact discipline: locate the field, verbalise the flaw, score on the act of *naming* it. The misconception "a defect is just a thing that broke" is replaced — over the course of one game — with the discipline "a defect is a traceable inconsistency in a documented field".
