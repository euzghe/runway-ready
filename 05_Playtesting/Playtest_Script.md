# 10-Minute Playtest Script (Week 3 In-Class Demo)

> Use this script when another group sits down to test *Runway Ready*. It is the timing
> contract for the Brief's "Playable 10-minute prototype" deliverable.

## Pre-game (≈ 2 minutes — runs while the playtesters arrive)

| Time     | Action                                                                |
|----------|-----------------------------------------------------------------------|
| 0:00–0:30| Hand each playtester the QuickReference card. Don't talk yet — let them read. |
| 0:30–1:30| Verbal pitch (script below — 60 seconds).                              |
| 1:30–2:00| Deal Studio Mats. Distribute starting cards (1 Brand Identity + 5 Outfits + 1 Roadmap + 1 Theme). |

### 60-second pitch script

> "You're the head of a fashion house. To send outfits down the runway, you have to write
> the same kind of paper trail a software test team writes — strategy, plan, design, cases,
> procedure. Every card has its ISO field name in italics. Half the Outfit deck has
> intentional defects in it. You score by writing documentation that survives execution and
> by catching defects in your rivals' documentation. You lose by skipping steps or by
> failing to name *which field* caused a defect when something breaks. Two rounds of
> roughly five minutes each, no event cards in this demo."

## In-game (≈ 7 minutes — Lite variant)

| Time      | Action                                                            |
|-----------|-------------------------------------------------------------------|
| 2:00–4:30 | Round 1. All players take turns: Plan → Design → Specify → Procedure → Rehearsal. |
| 4:30–5:00 | Fill Rehearsal Summary pads.                                      |
| 5:00–7:30 | Round 2. Same loop.                                               |
| 7:30–8:00 | Fill Rehearsal Summary pads.                                      |

## Post-game (≈ 2 minutes — feedback)

| Time      | Action                                                            |
|-----------|-------------------------------------------------------------------|
| 8:00–9:30 | Hand out the Feedback Form. Playtesters fill it in.               |
| 9:30–10:00| Quick verbal debrief (3 questions in `Feedback_Form.md`).         |

## Moderator instructions (the SunShine team member running the table)

You are not a player. You are:

1. The **rules referee** — answer rules questions, point at the Quick Reference if possible.
2. The **answer-key holder** — only you know which Outfit cards have planted flaws. Do not reveal.
3. The **timekeeper** — call the Inspection 60 seconds, the Read-Aloud cue, and round endings.
4. The **note-taker** — capture which mechanisms surprised playtesters (good and bad).

### What to look for during the session

These are the **observable signals** that the educational design is working:

- ✅ Did a playtester catch a flaw and name the field correctly during Inspection?
- ✅ Did a playtester verbalise a defect's originating field after a Wardrobe Malfunction?
- ✅ Did anyone try to skip a phase, and did the rest of the table catch it?
- ✅ Did anyone read a card aloud and *self-catch* a flaw before Inspection?
- ⚠️  Did anyone seem confused about the difference between Brand Identity and Roadmap?
- ⚠️  Did anyone try to "win" by playing more Outfits rather than safer ones?

The first four are evidence of mechanisms 1–5 in `04_GameLogic/Mistake_Reveal_Engine.md` working.
The last two are evidence we need to refine for Week 4.

## Hard time limits

- **Total session: 10:00 minutes.** Stop even mid-rehearsal at 10:00. Better to end on time than to overrun and fail the Brief's "10-minute prototype" criterion.
- If Round 1 overruns, **skip Round 2** and go straight to feedback. One full round is enough to reveal the mechanics.

## What to bring to the demo table

- 1 printed copy of QuickReference per playtester
- 4 Studio Mats (printed A4 landscape)
- The 96-card deck, sleeved if possible
- 1 d6
- 4 Rehearsal Summary pads (or scratch paper with the header pre-drawn)
- 1 stopwatch / phone timer for the 60-sec Inspection
- 4 Feedback Forms + pens
- The Answer Key (only the moderator's copy)
