# Learning Outcome Checklist

> One row per Week 2 learning objective. Each row maps the objective to the specific game mechanic that exercises it and the observable behaviour that proves it was achieved. This is the document the grader uses to score the **Learning Objectives (15 pts)** criterion.

## The four objectives (from Week 2 §2)

| # | Learning Objective                                   | Game mechanic that exercises it                                                          | Observable behaviour proving achievement                                       |
|---|------------------------------------------------------|------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|--------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| 1 | **Hierarchy Mapping** — internalise document dependency | Structural gating across 5 phases (`Turn_Flow_Diagram.md`).                              | Player attempts a phase skip, is corrected, and does not repeat the skip.       |
| 2 | **Traceability Application** — link defect to source field | Wardrobe Malfunction cards naming originating field; verbalisation rule (`Traceability_Logic.md`). | Player verbalises "Field X on Card Y said A but was contradicted by B" within 30 sec. |
| 3 | **Documentation Tailoring** — match doc depth to project scale | Roadmap variants (Capsule Drop vs Couture Gala) demand different Outfit counts and resource constraints. | Player completes a small Roadmap with 3 Outfits and a large Roadmap with 6, recognising both as valid 29119-3 plans. |
| 4 | **Root Cause Analysis** — defects are not random      | Mistake Reveal Engine mechanism 4: unflagged flaws *always* fail (`Mistake_Reveal_Engine.md` §3). | Player attributes their failure to a doc field, not to the dice roll, in the Rehearsal Summary. |

## Objective #5 (additional, surfaced through gameplay)

| # | Learning Objective                                   | Game mechanic                                                                            | Observable behaviour                                                            |
|---|------------------------------------------------------|------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| 5 | **Critical Reading of Documentation** — review another team's docs effectively | Inspection sub-step (60-sec rival flaw-spotting) + +2 Inspection Points reward. | Player correctly identifies a planted flaw on a rival's Outfit and names the field. |

## Measurability checkpoints

For each objective, the grader can check the **Rehearsal Summary pads** at game end:

- ✅ **Hierarchy** — every Summary line is filled in left-to-right (Outfit ID → Pass/Fail → Originating Field). A skipped column suggests hierarchy not internalised.
- ✅ **Traceability** — for every Fail row, the Originating Field column names a real ISO field, not "luck" or "broken".
- ✅ **Tailoring** — across 2 rounds, the Roadmaps the player chose used different scopes.
- ✅ **Root Cause** — no row attributes failure to "dice".
- ✅ **Critical Reading** — Inspection Points scored ≥ 1 across the game.

## Numeric target for the Week 3 demo

A single playtester completing 2 rounds should produce **at least 4 of the 5 observable behaviours**. If they produce all 5, the educational design is working. If they produce 0–1, we need to reduce the cognitive load (probably by simplifying the Theme deck or shortening the read-aloud step) for Week 4.
